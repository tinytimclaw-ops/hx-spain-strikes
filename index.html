Spanish Airport Strikes 2026

What you need to know to avoid travel disruption this Easter

⚠️

Ongoing Strike Action

Ground handling staff strikes began on 27 March 2026 and are set to continue throughout the Easter period and beyond. Check your flight status before travelling.

Ground handling staff at 12 major Spanish airports officially began industrial action on 27 March 2026, threatening to disrupt the plans of millions of domestic and international travellers during the busy Semana Santa (Holy Week) holiday period.

If you're planning to travel to or from Spain this Easter, here's everything you need to know about the strikes, which airports are affected, and how to protect your holiday.

What's happening?

Two major ground handling companies—Groundforce and Menzies—are experiencing labour disputes that have resulted in extensive strike action across Spain's busiest airports.

Groundforce strikes

An indefinite strike for Groundforce ground staff started on Monday 30 March, taking place during these time windows each day:

  • 5:00 AM – 7:00 AM
  • 11:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • 10:00 PM – 12:00 AM

Menzies strikes

Menzies has announced 24-hour full strikes covering the peak Easter travel dates:

  • 28–29 March
  • 2–6 April

Strike action will continue until 31 December 2026 if no agreement is reached between unions and management.

What disruption should I expect?

The most immediate risk for travellers isn't necessarily a cancelled flight, but a stranded suitcase. Baggage handling is the first service to fail during staffing shortages.

Likely delays and issues:

  • Longer check-in and bag drop queues
  • Baggage handling delays
  • Slower boarding and disembarkation processes
  • Potential flight delays or cancellations
  • Lost or delayed luggage

Travellers who can manage with carry-on luggage only will effectively bypass the highest risk.

5 tips to avoid disruption

1

Arrive extra early

Arrive at the airport 2-3 hours before departure for short-haul flights, and 3-4 hours for long-haul. This gives you buffer time for longer queues at check-in and bag drop.

2

Travel light with hand luggage only

Check in online and travel with cabin baggage only to minimise disruption. This is the single most effective way to protect yourself from baggage handling delays.

3

Stay informed

Monitor real-time updates from Aena (Spain's airport authority) and your airline. Sign up for flight status notifications via email or SMS.

4

Consider rebooking if possible

Airlines such as Iberia, Vueling, Ryanair, and easyJet have already begun issuing travel waivers, allowing passengers to rebook flights without penalties. Check your airline's policy.

5

Pack essentials in your hand luggage

If you must check a bag, pack medication, valuables, and a change of clothes in your carry-on in case your checked luggage is delayed.

Know your passenger rights

If your flight is cancelled or significantly delayed due to the Spanish ground staff strikes, you have rights under EU regulations.

EU261 compensation rules

Passengers are entitled to compensation of €250 to €600 depending on flight distance, provided the flight was cancelled or delayed by more than 3 hours.

Flights under 1,500km
€250
Flights 1,500–3,500km
€400
Flights over 3,500km
€600

Refund or rebooking

If your flight is cancelled, under passenger rights rules, airlines must either refund or rebook your flight—and you get to choose which option you prefer.

Care and assistance

For delays over 2 hours, airlines must provide:

  • Food and drink
  • Access to communication (phone calls, emails)
  • Hotel accommodation if an overnight stay is required
  • Transport between the airport and accommodation

Stay updated

The situation is evolving daily. For the latest information:

  • Check Aena's official website for real-time airport updates
  • Monitor your airline's website and social media channels
  • Sign up for flight status notifications
  • Check with your travel insurance provider about coverage

Most importantly: don't panic. While disruption is likely, advance preparation and flexibility will help you navigate the situation with minimal stress.
